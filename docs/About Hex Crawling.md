# About Hex Maps

Hex maps use 6-miles hexes, with a vertical (flat top), so they are 6 miles from top-face to bottom-face.

Each 6-mile hex contains 32 sq. miles, with a terrain type, d6 lairs and d6 features. The adventurers can either travel _through_ a hex, in which case they only discover what is on their direct route based on the terrain type, or they can do a thorough exploration of the hex for the purposes of surveying and finding everything in it. Generally the hex has a single terrain type, other than when called for by a lair or feature.

Key hexes with numbers xx.yy, where "x" are horizontal values increasing to the east and "y" are vertical values increasing to the south. Since they are vertical (flat top), odd-numbered columns are offset north by 1/2 a hex.

- 0505: Origin, starting location for the campaign
- 0504: North
- 0605: Northeast
- 0606: Southeast
- 0506: South
- 0406: Southwest
- 0405: Northwest

Key subhexes in the same way. You can generally keep this simple, so the starting location is subhex 0505 in hex 0505, but to be really pedantic you could refer to this hex as 0505.0505.

The starting location or "origin" is subhex 0505 inside hex 0505. Overall the 6-mile hex region 6 miles from north-to-south with subhexes ranging from 0303 to 0707. Beyond that the characters are entering a new 6-mile major hex.
